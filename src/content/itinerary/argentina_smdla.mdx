## Overview

A Lake District itinerary centered on San Martín de los Andes, Lake Lácar, Chapelco, and Lanín Volcano.
Compared to Bariloche, this area is smaller, quieter, and less urban.
It works well as a short standalone trip or as a continuation after Bariloche via the Ruta de los Siete Lagos.
San Martín de los Andes is both a calm base with enough nearby activities and a gateway to more remote volcanic and forest landscapes.

**Province:** Neuquén  
**Base:** San Martín de los Andes  
**Overall time:** 3–6 days; longer with Pucón or Villa Pehuenia  
**Main focus:** lakes, volcano views, forests, quiet Patagonia  
**Best season:** November–March for lakes and road trips; winter for Cerro Chapelco ski season

## Transportation

- A rented car adds flexibility for Lanín Volcano, remote beaches, Villa Pehuenia, and flexible weather planning
- Taxis are useful mostly for arrival and departure, but expensive for repeated sightseeing
- Tours exist for the main regional highlights, but there are fewer options than in Bariloche, so book or confirm ahead
