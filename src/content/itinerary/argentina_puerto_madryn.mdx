## Overview

A wildlife-focused itinerary centered on Puerto Madryn and Península Valdés,
one of South America’s most important marine ecosystems.

This region is defined by seasonality.
When conditions align, it offers some of the best coastal wildlife observation in the Southern Hemisphere.
Outside peak seasons, the experience is more limited and slower-paced.

**Province:** Chubut  
**Overall time:** 3–5 days  
**Main focus:** wildlife, marine life, open coastal landscapes  
**Best season:** August–October (southern right whales)

## Transportation

- Rented car is strongly recommended for flexibility and park access
- Tours are available but expensive and schedule-limited
- Public transport is very limited
- Taxis work well only within the city

---

## Connections

- Trelew (REL) → Ushuaia / El Calafate (seasonal direct flights)
- Night bus Puerto Madryn → Esquel (~10 hours)

## Notes

- This itinerary depends heavily on timing and season
- Whales are the main reason to visit — plan around them
- Wind and cool temperatures are common even in spring
