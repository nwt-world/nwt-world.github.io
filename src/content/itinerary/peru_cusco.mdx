## Overview

This is a first-visit Peru Andes itinerary focused on Cusco, the Sacred Valley, and Machu Picchu.

The route is intentionally staged for altitude:
Lima at sea level, Arequipa at a moderate altitude, Cusco for the first high-altitude adjustment,
then the lower Sacred Valley and Aguas Calientes before returning to Cusco.
The highest day trips, especially Rainbow Mountain, are best left until the end.

**Regions:** Lima, Arequipa, Cusco, Sacred Valley, Machu Picchu  
**Core minimum:** 9–10 nights with a pre-booked Machu Picchu ticket  
**Without a Machu Picchu ticket in advance:** 11–13 nights is safer  
**Main focus:** culture, archaeology, mountains  
**Best season:** May–September (dry season)

## Machu Picchu routes and tickets

This itinerary is designed for a first visit, so the route priority is simple: try to get Circuit 2 first, then use Circuit 1 as the best panoramic fallback, and Circuit 3 only if the better first-visit routes are gone.

### Route priority for a first visit

- **Circuit 2 / Route 2-A or 2-B: best first choice**  
  The most balanced first-visit option. It combines the classic Machu Picchu view with a longer walk through the citadel area, which is why these tickets are usually the hardest to get.

- **Circuit 1 / Route 1-B: best panoramic fallback**  
  Good if the main priority is the classic upper view and photos. It is shorter and more viewpoint-focused, with less time inside the citadel itself.

- **Circuit 3 / Route 3-B: acceptable fallback**  
  Lower route with more of the lower citadel area, but without the classic upper panorama. Better than skipping Machu Picchu, but not the ideal first ticket.

- **Route 1-A / Machu Picchu Mountain and Route 3-A / Huayna Picchu: hiking-focused options**  
  Choose these if the hike itself is a priority. They can give excellent views, but they are more physically demanding and more weather-dependent.

For a first visit, the practical order is:

1. Circuit 2  
2. Circuit 1  
3. Circuit 3  
4. Hiking routes only if you specifically want the hike

### Best timing in Aguas Calientes

The rushed version is possible:
- Morning train to Aguas Calientes
- Machu Picchu visit
- Evening train back

This works only if:
- You already have the right Machu Picchu ticket
- Train times match your entrance time
- You are comfortable with a long, tightly planned day

The calmer version is better:
- 1 night in Aguas Calientes if you already have the ticket
- 3 nights if you want two full Machu Picchu chances or need to use the in-person ticket system

This is more expensive, but Aguas Calientes is warmer and more pleasant for rest than Cusco, and the extra time gives a better chance of clear mountain views.

### Backup option: buy in person in Aguas Calientes

The official in-person system keeps a daily allocation of 1,000 tickets at the Centro Cultural in Machupicchu Pueblo / Aguas Calientes.

Important:
- Tickets are for the following day
- Sales start at 6:00 and continue until 22:00 or until tickets sell out
- Route choice is not guaranteed
- Check the official remaining-ticket tracker before relying on this option

For Circuit 2, do not rely on arriving in the afternoon.

Safer slow plan without an advance ticket:
- Day 1 – arrive in Aguas Calientes
- Day 2 – go early, ideally around 6:00–7:00, and buy the best available ticket for the next day
- Day 3 – visit Machu Picchu
- Day 4 – return to Cusco or continue the route

Extra safety plan:
- If any acceptable fallback online ticket is available, buy it first
- Then use the in-person system to try for Circuit 2
- This can give you one guaranteed visit and one attempt at the better first-visit route

### Consettur bus from Aguas Calientes

The Consettur shuttle bus is the standard way to reach the Machu Picchu entrance from Aguas Calientes.

How to buy:
- Online through the official bus ticket site
- In person in Aguas Calientes

Practical advice:
- Buy the bus ticket the evening before your Machu Picchu visit
- This avoids wasting time in the morning
- The ticket is date-based, not tied to one exact departure bus
- Join the bus line around 1 hour before your Machu Picchu entrance time
- Morning lines can be long, but they usually move steadily

Walking up is possible, but it adds effort before the visit and is not needed for most travelers.

### Train luggage

Machu Picchu trains are not designed for large luggage.

Inca Rail:
- Allows only one small hand-luggage item
- Large 20–23 kg suitcases are not allowed on normal Machu Picchu trains

PeruRail:
- Small hand luggage is included
- Extra luggage can be added for a fee, including larger checked luggage

Storage:
- Both PeruRail and Inca Rail provide luggage storage options in Ollantaytambo
- The safest plan is to leave large luggage in Cusco or Ollantaytambo and take only a small backpack to Aguas Calientes

## What to book in advance

Highest priority:
- Machu Picchu ticket, especially Circuit 2
- Train to / from Aguas Calientes
- Aguas Calientes accommodation

Also useful:
- Consettur bus ticket, ideally the evening before the visit
- Domestic flights Lima → Arequipa, Arequipa → Cusco, Cusco → Lima

The rest can usually be booked locally or a few days ahead.
