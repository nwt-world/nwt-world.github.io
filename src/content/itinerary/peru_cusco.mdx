## Overview

This is Peru’s core itinerary, combining volcanic landscapes, Inca heritage, and
carefully staged altitude acclimatization.

The route is designed to reduce altitude stress by starting lower and moving higher gradually,
while keeping logistics simple and reliable.

**Regions:** Lima, Arequipa, Cusco, Sacred Valley  
**Overall time:** 6–8 days  
**Main focus:** culture, archaeology, mountains  
**Ranking:** top cultural itinerary  
**Best season:** May–September (dry season)

## Transportation

- Flights are essential for long distances (Lima–Arequipa–Cusco)
- Trains are recommended for Machu Picchu
- Group tours simplify Sacred Valley logistics
- Taxis and walking work well inside cities

---

## Altitude

- Arequipa (~2,300 m) is ideal for initial adjustment
- Cusco (~3,400 m) requires rest and pacing
- Sacred Valley and Machu Picchu are lower and easier
- Avoid overloading the first Cusco day

---

## Notes

- Book Machu Picchu tickets and trains well in advance
- This itinerary balances culture with altitude safety
- Can be extended with Lake Titicaca or Colca Canyon if needed
